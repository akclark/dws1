HTML Elements and Their Definitions:

<h1>
Header 1 tag - the largest and most prominent of the header tags.

<h2>
Header 2 tag - the next step down from the <h1> tag, usually used for a subheader.

<h3> through <h6>
Header 3 - 6 tags - further smaller and less obtrusive versions of the h tag.

<p>
Creates a new paragraph on the page.

<b>
Makes the enclosed text bold.

<i>
Makes the enlcosed text italicized.

<sup>
Raises the enclosed text above the surrounding text like a superscript.

<sub>
Lowers the enclosed text below the surrounding text like a subscript.

<br />
Inserts a line break between items, is a self closed element.

<hr />
Inserts a line (Horizontal Rule) on your page.
<strong>
Used to indicate importance, usually interpreted onscreen as bold.

<em>
Used to indicate emphasis on the enclosed words, usually onscreen as italics.

<blockquote>
Used to quote a larger block of text, often indented on screen.

<q>
Used to put quotes on smaller pieces of text than <blockquote>

<abbr>
Used to define an abbreviation or (in HTML5) an acronym.

<cite>
Used to indicate the title of a book or other work.

<dfn>
Used to indicate the first time a term is defined.

<address>
Usually in the footer, this tag is used to contain the contact information of the pages' author.

<ins>
Used most often in conjunction with <del> it indicates the replacement with underlining.

<del>
Used most often in conjunction with <ins> it indicates the text being replaced with strikethrough.

<s>
Crosses the enclosed text out ia strikethrough.

HTML List Elements:


<ol>
An Ordered List, it uses numbers to indicate the order of the items, usually indented.

<ul>
An Unordered List, usually indicated with only bullets, usually indented.

<li>
List Item - the enclosed item appears in the list, this tag should always be within either an <ul>, or a <ol>.

<dl>
Indicates a definition list follows.

<dt>
Indicates the title or term that is being defined.

<dd>
Indicates the definition of the term.
